# ✍️ Non-repudiation
**Course:** CompTIA Security+ | Professor Messer  
**Domain:** 1 — General Security Concepts  
**Topic:** Non-repudiation, Integrity, and Digital Signatures

---

## 🧭 Overview
Non-repudiation ensures that the sender of a message cannot deny having sent it. Much like a handwritten signature on a legal contract, cryptography provides high assurance of **authenticity** and **integrity**, making a transaction legally and technically "undeniable."

---

## 🏗️ The Two Pillars of Non-repudiation
To achieve non-repudiation, we need to prove two things simultaneously:

### 1. Proof of Integrity (The "What")
We must verify that the data received is exactly what was sent, without a single character being changed.
* **The Hash:** A short string of text (fingerprint) created from the plaintext.
* **Message Digest:** If the data changes, the hash changes completely.
* **Example:** Project Gutenberg Encyclopedia (8.1MB). Changing just one letter in that massive haystack of data results in a completely different hash value.

### 2. Proof of Origin (The "Who")
We must verify exactly who sent the message. This is also called **Authentication**.
* **The Private Key:** Only the sender has this key. 
* **The Public Key:** Anyone can use this to verify the signature.
* **Digital Signatures:** By combining a hash with a private key, we link the "Proof of Integrity" to a specific person.

---

## 🛠️ How a Digital Signature Works (Behind the Scenes)
When you click "Add Digital Signature," a complex process happens instantly. Let's look at the "Alice and Bob" workflow:

### Step 1: The Sender (Alice)
1. **Hashing:** Alice creates a message ("You're hired, Bob"). Her computer runs a hashing algorithm to create a unique **Message Digest**.
2. **Encryption:** Alice encrypts that hash using **her Private Key**.
3. **Delivery:** She sends the original message + the encrypted hash (Digital Signature) to Bob.

### Step 2: The Receiver (Bob)
1. **Decryption:** Bob uses **Alice's Public Key** to decrypt the digital signature. This gives him the original hash Alice created.
2. **Independent Hashing:** Bob takes the message he received and runs it through the *same* hashing algorithm himself.
3. **The Comparison:** Bob compares his calculated hash with the hash he decrypted from Alice.
   * ✅ **Match:** The data is perfect (Integrity) and it definitely came from Alice (Origin).
   * ❌ **No Match:** Either the data was tampered with, or it wasn't Alice who signed it!

## 🧠 Self-Test Questions
> **Q1: If Bob decrypts a digital signature using Alice's public key and the hashes match, what is guaranteed?**
> <details><summary>Click for Answer</summary><b>Non-repudiation:</b> Bob knows the data hasn't changed (Integrity) and Alice cannot deny she sent it (Origin).</details>

> **Q2: Does a standard hash (like MD5 or SHA-256) provide non-repudiation by itself?**
> <details><summary>Click for Answer</summary><b>No.</b> A hash only provides Integrity. It proves the data hasn't changed, but it doesn't prove <i>who</i> sent it.</details>
